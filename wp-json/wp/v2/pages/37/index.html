driftoff is a research project.<\/h2>\n\n\n\n

driftoff is a prototype app to support your sleep. It was developed alongside university students. This project is led by Dan Archer, a Public Health PhD candidate at Te Herenga Waka | Victoria University of Wellington.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

<\/div>\n\n\n\n

By using this app you will help to give feedback about its usability.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

This research has been approved by the Te Herenga Waka\u2014Victoria University of Wellington Human Ethics Committee #2024\/HE000074.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

<\/div>\n\n\n\n

Ka p\u0113hea t\u014d \u0101whina mai?<\/h2>\n\n\n\n

How can you help?<\/h2>\n\n\n\n

This project is seeking to understand how to make mobile health apps more appealing. The information from this project will be used to make this app better, as well as in my PhD dissertation, publications and conferences.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

<\/div>\n\n\n\n

Before you start, you will be asked some question about your sleep and also your current wellbeing. You\u2019ll be asked these questions every week for four weeks along with an opportunity to provide feedback. Your usage of the app will also be recorded. After four weeks you will be sent a questionnaire about your experiences. You may also be invited to be interviewed about your experience.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

<\/div>\n\n\n\n

You can withdraw your participation at any time<\/strong> by closing and uninstalling the app. You can also request a copy of your data, or to have your data deleted through the app or by emailing the researcher. This must be done before [date]<\/p>\n\n\n\n
